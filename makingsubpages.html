Making My Subpages

After creating our homepage, we began to work on our subpages. Before getting into actually creating the subpages, there were a few things we needed to put into consideration.

First, we reviewed the flowchart we had to decide what subpages we had to make.

The subpages that we have on the first level are "Our Products," "Local Products," and "Local Benefits" with one extra page under Our Products. The overall theme that we want to convey is simplicity. One of the biggest messages that we want to convey with the new website is that it is authentic, and the theme of simplicity helps convey this. Most visitors of this page think that the website is a counterfeit because it looks too much like websites such as Alibaba or taobao.

However, instead of making a sample page of a brand page, we are deciding to create a product page instead. This is because we are make a demo, and the brand pages look similar to "Our Products" and "Locally Popular Products" anyway. We will also split the About Us/Local Benefits page into two different pages, because they are not completely relevant. Users who are only interested in one of the topics would be more at ease if the information is not clumped up together.

 

This is the wireframe that we created for our subpages. We will keep the logo and the horizontal navigation bar from the homepage. The space in the middle is a editable region that will be a catalogue of products for the pages "Our Products" and "Local Products" and it will be a text page without the photos for the "Local Benefits" and "About Us" page.

 

This is the actual file template for all my Subpages.

The boxes are editable regions in which different photos can be put in. The bigger box is an optional editable region, which can be deleted in whole if the webpage is not a product page and the boxes are not needed.
